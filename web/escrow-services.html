Escrow Services

Escrow guidance for high-value peer transactions where staged release and objective evidence are required.

Escrow Model

Recommended model is 2-of-3 multisig between buyer, seller, and neutral adjudicator to reduce unilateral risk.

Milestone Structure

Define milestones before funding: deposit, inspection window, handoff confirmation, and release conditions.

Required Artifacts

Maintain signed agreement, ID verification evidence, title documents, condition records, and release/refund criteria.

Failure Handling

When a milestone fails, move to dispute resolution with documented evidence and agreed arbitration rules.

Lower Risk

Funds are locked until milestones are met, reducing exposure for both parties.

Clear Milestones

Define deposit, inspection, handoff, and release conditions up front to avoid ambiguity.

Transparent Audit

Maintain a clear trail of signatures, approvals, and BTC txids to resolve disputes quickly.

Who holds the keys?

In 2-of-3 multisig, the buyer and seller each hold a key, with a neutral third party as adjudicator.

What if a milestone is disputed?

The adjudicator reviews evidence and co-signs for release or refund according to the agreement.
